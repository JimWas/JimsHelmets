CIRCULAR SAFETY

Building the infrastructure for a lifetime of protection. In our model, a helmet isn't a product—it's a service that evolves with the child.

The Growth Paradox

The primary reason parents in Cambodia avoid purchasing high-quality helmets is the "Growth Gap." Between ages 2 and 12, a child's head circumference increases by approximately 10cm. A properly fitted helmet only accommodates a 2cm variance.

The Reality

"Why buy a $25 helmet today when it will be dangerously tight in 18 months? Most parents wait until the child is 'grown up,' leaving them unprotected during their most vulnerable years."

The "Swap" Infrastructure

We replace the traditional retail model with a three-pillar circular system designed for local scalability and trust.

🏭

Safety Hubs

Centralized facilities in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap that handle professional cleaning, foam inspection, and ECE-standard shell testing.

🏫

School Spokes

Every participating primary school acts as a collection point. Parents drop off the "small" and pick up the "medium" during morning enrollment.

📱

Digital Triage

A simple Telegram-based bot tracks each helmet's unique QR code, alerting parents when a growth-swap is due based on the child's age.

Unlocking Economic Access

The traditional "Buy New" model costs a family ~$100 over a child's growth cycle (4 helmets). Our Swap Infrastructure reduces this to a one-time $10 membership and a $2 swap fee.

The Refurbishment Cycle

Step 1

Return & Credit

Family returns outgrown helmet; receives credit for next size.

Step 2

Deep Sanitize

Medical-grade UV and steam cleaning of all surfaces.

Step 3

Structural Scan

Ultrasonic test for micro-cracks in EPS foam liner.

Step 4

Re-Issue

New padding inserted; helmet certified for next user.
