The "Helmet Gap"

A quality, ECE-certified child helmet costs $20 - $25. A family must save for two months just to buy protection that will be outgrown in 18 months.

The Outgrowth Obsolescence

Children's heads grow rapidly during the most vulnerable riding ages (2 to 10 years). Parents avoid the investment because "next year it won't fit," leading to the dangerous use of adult helmets (too loose) or no helmet at all.

The "SafeHeads" Subscription Strategy

1. Initial Subsidized Entry

Family pays $5 (instead of $20) for the first certified helmet, sponsored by CSR grants.

1

2. The Growth Swap

When the child outgrows the helmet, the family returns it and pays a $2 "Refresh Fee" for the next size up.

2

3. Refurbishment Cycle

SafeHeads technicians inspect returned helmets. Non-impacted shells are deep-cleaned, fitted with new liners, and re-issued.

3

Safety shouldn't have an expiration date.

By solving the "growth cost," we remove the final excuse for why Cambodia's children ride unprotected.
