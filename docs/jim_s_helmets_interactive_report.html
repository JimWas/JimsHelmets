The Cambodia Road Crisis

Road traffic accidents are the #1 cause of death for Cambodians aged 5-24. While motorcycle ownership is the backbone of the economy, safety infrastructure has not kept pace with population growth.

📊 Fatalities by Vehicle Type

Motorcycle riders and passengers account for 75% of all annual deaths (approx. 1,976 annually). Data sourced from WHO (2023).

⚖ The "Safety Gap" (Usage Rates)

While adult driver enforcement is improving, child passenger helmet usage remains dangerously low (<10% in rural areas).

The School-Based Model

We don't just hand out helmets. We build a culture of safety. Our primary delivery vehicle is the **Safety Assembly**, where education is the prerequisite for protection.

The 5-Year Journey

Year 1: The Proof of Concept

Launch 1,000 helmet pilot in Phnom Penh. Establish the U.S. 501(c)(3) and Cambodian INGO status.

Year 2-3: Provincial Scaling

Expand to 5 provinces including Siem Reap and Battambang. Target: 10,000 helmets per year. Build corporate coalition with local banks (ABA) and telcos (Smart).

Year 4-5: National Advocacy

Leverage data to lobby for mandatory passenger helmet laws for minors. Goal: Integrated road safety curriculum in all primary schools nationwide.
