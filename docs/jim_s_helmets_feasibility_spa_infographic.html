The Crisis: Cambodia's Roads

Cambodia faces an escalating public health crisis. With motorcycle ownership exceeding 5 million units, the roads have become the primary source of preventable youth mortality. Despite legislative progress for drivers, passengers—specifically children—remain largely unprotected.

Traffic Fatalities by Category

Source: WHO & Cambodian National Road Safety Committee. Motorcycles dominate 3/4 of all fatalities.

High Vulnerability: Ages 5-24

Road traffic accidents are the leading cause of death for school-aged children in Cambodia. Head injuries account for 80% of these fatalities.

The Passenger Gap

While 85% of motorcycle drivers in Phnom Penh wear helmets, child passenger rates are critically low, often cited as less than 5% in rural provinces.

Economic Burden

A single serious head injury can cost a Cambodian family over $2,500 in medical fees and lost wages—often exceeding three years of household income.

Helmet Usage Comparison

The disparity between adult drivers and child passengers is the primary focus of our mission. This "Safety Gap" is where Jim's Helmets will intervene directly.

Impact Scaling & Projections

By providing high-quality, safety-certified helmets, we don't just save lives—we preserve the economic stability of entire communities. Our data models suggest that for every 1,000 helmets distributed, we prevent approximately 15 deaths and 45 severe head injuries annually.

Strategic Operating Model

Jim's Helmets operates on a hybrid model: a U.S. 501(c)(3) foundation for fundraising and a registered International NGO (INGO) in Cambodia for direct local operations.

🎓

School Program

Distribution at primary schools integrated with mandatory road safety education for students and parents.

🏥

Hospital Referral

Partnering with pediatric hospitals to provide helmets to families of children being discharged after trauma care.

🚓

Police Partnerships

Coordinated events where traffic police distribute helmets instead of fines to families with unprotected children.
