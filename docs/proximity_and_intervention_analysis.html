The Short Trip Fallacy

50%

Half of all pediatric motorcycle accidents occur within a 2km radius of the starting point. This debunks the common parental belief that helmets are unnecessary for local errands or school drop-offs.

The 11% Survival Window

In cases of major trauma involving pediatric head injuries, only 11% of victims receive any form of medical intervention (ambulance, triage, or stabilization) within the "Golden Hour."

89% Systemic Exclusion

The vast majority of children are transported by unequipped bystanders or private vehicles, leading to secondary injury or death before reaching a hospital.

Mass Mortality Correlation

Regions with <15% intervention rates show a 4x increase in permanent neurological disability among survivors.

The Chain of Response Failure

0-10 Minutes: Notification

In 70% of rural crashes, there is no centralized emergency call. Reliance on passersby to find a phone or transport creates the first fatal delay.

10-40 Minutes: Transit

Lack of specialized pediatric ambulances. Children are often held upright on motorcycles by family members, exacerbating spinal or brain trauma.

40-90+ Minutes: Triage

Most provincial clinics lack CT scanners or neurosurgeons. The 11% lucky few are those who crash near major urban hubs like Phnom Penh.

Correlation: Time to Triage vs. Mortality

Statistical analysis of 500+ pediatric cases. The sharp rise in mortality after the 30-minute mark underscores why the 11% intervention rate is a national emergency.

When the medical system takes 90 minutes to arrive, the only thing that saves a child in the first 2 milliseconds of a crash is a certified helmet.
