Pillar-by-Pillar Research MethodologyPillar A: Direct Medical Cost Reductions & Public Health SavingsThe Objective: Quantify the immediate and long-term medical expenditures saved by preventing Traumatic Brain Injuries (TBIs).Research Questions:What is the average cost of acute care for a pediatric TBI at major public safety nets (e.g., Kantha Bopha Children’s Hospital, Calmette Hospital)?How do ICU stay-durations and surgical costs scale between helmeted vs. unhelmeted crash victims?Econometric Equation:The saved medical cost ($C_{saved}$) per averted injury is modeled as:$$C_{saved} = P_{injury} \times (1 - \alpha) \times (C_{acute} + C_{rehab})$$Where:$P_{injury}$ is the baseline probability of an accident-induced pediatric head injury over a $5$-year helmet lifespan.$\alpha$ is the helmet effectiveness coefficient in reducing severe head injuries (established in medical literature as $\approx 0.30$, representing a $70\%$ reduction).$C_{acute}$ and $C_{rehab}$ represent localized emergency, neurosurgical, and physical rehabilitation costs.Pillar B: Lifetime Earnings Preservation (Human Capital Value)The Objective: Measure the preserved economic productivity of a child who avoids death or permanent cognitive/motor disability.Research Questions:What is the net present value (NPV) of a Cambodian child's future labor contribution to the transitioning economy?How does pediatric TBI impact high school graduation rates and subsequent lifetime tax contributions?Econometric Equation:We calculate the Net Present Value of Lifetime Earnings ($PV_{LTE}$) using the discount rate $r$ (calibrated to Cambodia's central bank baseline) from the age of entry into the workforce ($t = 18$) to retirement ($t = 65$):$$PV_{LTE} = \sum_{t=18}^{65} \frac{W_t \times (1 + g)^{t-18}}{(1 + r)^{t-18}}$$Where:$W_t$ is the average annual wage adjusted for economic sector growth.$g$ is the projected annual GDP wage growth rate for Cambodia (historically $\approx 5.5\% - 7\%$).$r$ is the social discount rate (typically set at $3\% - 5\%$ for public health investments).Pillar C: Household Debt Avoidance & Poverty PreventionThe Objective: Model how preventing a TBI stops families from falling into catastrophic debt traps and land-selling cycles.Research Questions:What percentage of rural Cambodian families resort to informal microfinance loans (often with interest rates exceeding $30\%$ annually) to pay for emergency trauma care?What is the economic ripple effect of parents withdrawing older siblings from school to work and offset medical debts?Methodology:Conduct retrospective financial surveys of families admitted to trauma centers. Track the "poverty multiplier effect" of medical shocks, measuring long-term household consumption drops.Pillar D: The Economics of Happiness & Well-Being (DALYs)The Objective: Quantify the intangible value of a healthy, pain-free life using internationally recognized development metrics.Research Questions:How many Disability-Adjusted Life Years (DALYs) are saved per $1,000$ helmets distributed?What is the economic valuation of a single Cambodian DALY using the WHO standard (typically valued at $1\times$ to $3\times$ GDP per capita)?Econometric Equation:$$\text{DALY} = \text{YLL} \text{ (Years of Life Lost)} + \text{YLD} \text{ (Years Lived with Disability)}$$Given Cambodia’s nominal GDP per capita ($\approx \$2,000$ in 2024), saving just $1$ child's life ($60$ years of lost life avoided, or $60$ YLL) returns:$$\text{Economic Value} = 60 \times \$2,000 = \$120,000$$3. Risk and Mitigation MatrixRisk: Hospital records may lack granular detail on whether a child was helmeted at the moment of impact.Mitigation: Focus the research on "Expected Regional Compliance Statistics" to model the comparative probabilities, and supplement with active prospective tracking of our own pilot schools.Risk: The high inflation or volatility of the Cambodian Riel could distort long-term wage calculations.Mitigation: Conduct all lifetime productivity calculations in USD (the dominant commercial currency in Cambodia) and stress-test the model with a range of discount rates ($3\%, 5\%, 8\%$).
