Physics of Fragility

A child's brain is not a small version of an adult's. It is more liquid, less shielded, and breaks at speeds we consider "safe."

The Fatal Threshold

90 G's

While an adult skull might withstand 120-150 Gs, 90 Gs is the critical limit where pediatric brain tissue begins to tear (Diffuse Axonal Injury).

The Asphalt Reality

200+ G's

A simple fall from a motorcycle seat at 30 km/h onto concrete generates over 200 Gs of instantaneous deceleration.

Velocity vs. Impact Intensity

G-force doesn't increase linearly with speed; it increases exponentially. A "small" increase in motorcycle speed results in a massive surge in brain trauma potential.

Biological Disparity

Brain Water Content 88% (Child)

Higher water = higher risk of swelling and axonal shearing.

Skull Thickness 2.1mm (Child)

Child skulls are 3x thinner and more pliable than adults.

"The child's brain moves inside the skull like a liquid, not a solid. In a 100G impact, the brain strikes the inner skull with enough force to cause permanent cognitive loss."

THE 2-MILLISECOND SHIELD

A helmet doesn't stop the impact; it slows it down. By compressing the EPS foam liner for just 2 extra milliseconds, a helmet drops the G-force from 200+ (Fatal) to under 60 (Survivable).
