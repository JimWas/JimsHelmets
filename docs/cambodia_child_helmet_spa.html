The Enforcement Disconnect

Since the 2016 Road Traffic Law, adult drivers face fines for not wearing helmets. However, enforcement for passengers—specifically children—remains lax. This visualization highlights the massive safety disparity between the person controlling the motorcycle and the child riding behind them.

"Parents often view helmets as a tool to avoid fines rather than a tool to save lives. Because police rarely stop children, the helmet stays at home."

Takeaway: Over 90% of children are currently exposed to fatal head trauma during daily commutes.

Why the Hesitation?

Cost (35%): High-quality pediatric helmets are often too expensive for rural families.

The "Short Trip" Myth (40%): Many parents believe low speeds on local roads don't require protection.

SafeHeads Cambodia Strategic Roadmap

Phase 1: Local Manufacturing

Partnering with regional factories to produce affordable, ECE-certified helmets specifically sized for Khmer children.

Phase 2: School-Based Distribution

Integrating helmet distribution into the primary school registration process across 5 key provinces.

Phase 3: Policy Advocacy

Working with the National Road Safety Committee to mandate child helmet use with strict roadside penalties.

Target Outcomes

  • 500,000 helmets distributed by 2028
  • 80% child compliance in urban hubs
  • $45M saved in medical/productivity costs
