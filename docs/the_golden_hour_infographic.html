The Survival Decay Curve

This data visualizes the probability of a "Good Outcome" (survival without severe disability) following a high-speed motorcycle head impact without a helmet. Every minute of delay in specialized neuro-triage costs lives.

The Insight: By the time a rural Cambodian child reaches a facility capable of a CT scan (usually 2+ hours), the survival probability has already dropped below 15%. This makes pre-hospital prevention the only viable medical strategy.

! The Chain of Systemic Delay

1. The "Bystander" Delay (10-20 mins)

Lack of centralized 119 dispatch in many areas means reliance on private citizens to find transport.

2. The "Logistics" Gap (30-60 mins)

Severe traffic congestion in Phnom Penh or poor road quality in rural zones stalls ambulance movement.

3. The "Financial" Triage (15-30 mins)

Time lost at private clinics that lack the equipment for head trauma but require payment before transfer to public specialists.

4. The Surgical Queue (Total: >180 mins)

Result: The Golden Hour is missed by over 200% in 85% of pediatric cases.
