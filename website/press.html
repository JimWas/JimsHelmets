Philadelphia, PA — June 2026

Philadelphia Entrepreneur Launches Nonprofit to Put Certified Helmets on Cambodia's Unprotected Child Motorcycle Passengers

Jim's Helmets pairs certified tropical child helmets with school-based behavior change programs targeting a country where fewer than one percent of child motorcycle passengers ride with protection.

Jim Washkau, a Philadelphia-based marketing professional and Temple University graduate, has launched Jim's Helmets, a nonprofit organization dedicated to reducing preventable brain injury among children in Cambodia by distributing certified child motorcycle helmets through school-based safety programs.

The initiative grew directly from Washkau's time living in Cambodia, where he experienced the country's motorcycle-dependent transportation culture firsthand. Cambodia's roads carry families of three, four, and five on single motorcycles — often including infants and school-age children — with little to no protective gear. Road traffic injuries are among the leading causes of death and permanent disability for Cambodian children and adolescents, and baseline observations in some regions show child passenger helmet use rates below one percent.

"I watched families making impossible choices every day — not because they didn't care about their children, but because the helmets were too expensive, too hot, too poorly fitted, or simply not available. The barrier isn't awareness. It's access, trust, and habit."

— Jim Washkau, Founder, Jim's Helmets

Jim's Helmets is not a simple gear-donation program. The organization's model pairs each certified child helmet with a 12-week school-gate behavior change campaign: parent workshops that address common safety myths, public handover ceremonies with local school and community leaders, teacher-reinforced road safety curriculum, and structured follow-up observations to verify that protection becomes a daily habit rather than a one-time event.

The organization has launched a founding campaign targeting 1,000 certified child helmets for an initial school-based pilot. At an estimated $25 per child — covering the certified tropical helmet, fitting, and parent education materials — the campaign represents a direct, measurable investment in child survival. Helmets are sourced to meet recognized safety certification standards, with an emphasis on low weight and passive airflow ventilation suited to Cambodia's climate.

Washkau draws a deliberate parallel to the seatbelt adoption story: a safety behavior that once faced resistance and skepticism became normalized through better equipment, repeated education, and consistent social expectation. Jim's Helmets is designed to accelerate that arc for Cambodian children — before the next school ride.

"A helmet doesn't just protect a child's head. It protects the family from the medical debt, land loss, and sibling dropout that follow a catastrophic injury. For $25, a donor can prevent a chain reaction that can last years."

— Jim Washkau, Founder, Jim's Helmets

The founding campaign is open now at jimshelmets.org/donate. The organization is actively seeking founding donors, school and NGO partners in Cambodia, and corporate sponsors interested in child road safety impact.

About Jim's Helmets

Jim's Helmets is a nonprofit organization founded by Jim Washkau to address the child motorcycle helmet crisis in Cambodia through certified helmet distribution and school-based behavior change programs. The organization's 12-week school-gate model pairs certified tropical child helmets with parent education, teacher reinforcement, and follow-up usage measurement. Jim's Helmets is headquartered in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Learn more at jimshelmets.org.
