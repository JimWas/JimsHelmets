The crisis

Cambodian children ride through a preventable brain-injury crisis.

Motorcycles are the family vehicle across Cambodia. The research in this project shows the danger is not abstract: child passengers ride daily with very low helmet use, limited emergency response, and fragile household finances if a crash happens.

85%-98%

of Cambodia's active vehicle fleet is motorcycles in the research plan.

22%

11%

of injured children reach medical care within 30 minutes, according to the plan.

$1B

Why children

A low-speed fall can become a lifetime injury.

Children are not small adults. Their skulls are thinner, their heads are proportionally larger, and a traumatic brain injury can interrupt the entire arc of brain development. Prevention is the strongest protection when emergency response can be slow.

Not parental indifference

The gap is built from access, heat, myths, cost, and routine.

The research is clear that families often value safety but face daily tradeoffs. A nonprofit that wants lasting helmet use has to remove practical barriers and make protection a visible community norm.

The short-trip fallacy

Many school and market trips feel too familiar to be dangerous, even though crash risk starts close to home.

The passenger gap

Helmet laws and enforcement have historically changed adult driver behavior faster than child passenger behavior.

The cost shock

Families earning around $150-$200 per month may choose a $2 plastic cap over certified protection unless donors remove the cost barrier.

Why invest

Prevention is cheaper than rescue, debt, disability, and dropout.

A helmet program protects more than a head. It helps preserve school attendance, household land, caregiver income, and a child's future productivity.

Medical costs avoided

Neuro-trauma care can equal a large share of a rural household's annual income.

Debt traps prevented

Emergency loans can lead to land loss, school dropout, and years of compounding hardship.

Human capital protected

Every avoided severe brain injury preserves years of education, work, and family stability.
