The program

A helmet becomes protection when it becomes a habit.

The program is designed as a 12-week school pathway, not a one-day giveaway. Education, social proof, and follow-up are what turn equipment into behavior.

12-week pathway

From school audit to daily helmet use.

1

School selection

Prioritize high-risk corridors, public schools, and campuses with heavy motorcycle commuting.

2

Baseline audit

Measure commute patterns, helmet use, school-gate flow, and student sizing needs.

3

Teacher training

Equip teachers to reinforce road-safety routines and help measure fit and compliance.

4

Parent covenant

Parents attend a short workshop and commit to every-ride helmet use.

5

Handover day

Local leaders and police make safety public, visible, and socially reinforced.

6

Curriculum

Students learn simple rules like "No Helmet, No Ride" through repeated school messaging.

7

Follow-up

Track usage at 2 weeks, 12 weeks, and 3-4 months, then report results to donors.

Parents learn the why

Workshops debunk the soft-neck and head-growth myths while showing correct fit and strap use.

Schools create routine

School-gate checks and classroom reinforcement make wearing a helmet visible and normal.

Donors see outcomes

Impact reports should include helmets distributed, schools reached, workshops completed, and follow-up helmet-use rates.

Partner model

Built for schools, local leaders, and founding sponsors.

The site should invite schools, corporate sponsors, traffic-safety allies, and foundations into a practical operating model with clear roles and visible reporting.
